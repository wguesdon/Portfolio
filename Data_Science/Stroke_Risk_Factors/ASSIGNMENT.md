# Data Science Programming Assignment: Factors Influencing Stroke

This assignment explores a healthcare dataset and is purposely left vague.
There is no single correct answer — the approach is left to your discretion.
The assignment is intended to be completed well within 48 hours.

---

## Dataset

Download `train_2v.csv` from the Kaggle stroke dataset:
[https://www.kaggle.com/asaumya/healthcare-dataset-stroke-data](https://www.kaggle.com/asaumya/healthcare-dataset-stroke-data)

---

## Task

Using a programming language of your choice (preferably **R or Python**),
complete **one** of the following options using generally accessible libraries:

- **Option 1 — Supervised statistical learning**
  Use statistical learning (with or without dimensionality reduction) to
  identify the combination of features more likely to be associated with stroke.

- **Option 2 — Unsupervised statistical learning**
  Use statistical learning (with or without dimensionality reduction) to
  identify different populations in the data and their susceptibility to stroke.

- **Option 3 — Causal inference**
  Use causal inference to identify the features more likely to have a causal
  effect on stroke.

- **Option 4 — Open choice**
  Build another form of statistical model that identifies relevant features.

---

## Report

A brief report (**no more than 3 pages including plots**) covering:

1. Your choice of data pre-processing and justification
2. Steps taken to account for potential class imbalance
3. Your analytical choice compared to alternative approaches
4. The level of uncertainty associated with your results

Reference any online code your analysis is based on.
Aim for compact, efficient, documented code.

---

1. Report (PDF, HTML, or similar)

3. Environment documentation sufficient to reproduce results with minimum effort
